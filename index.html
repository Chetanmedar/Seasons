Seasons of Indian Agriculture

Rainy Season (Monsoon / Kharif)

The Rainy Season coincides with the arrival of monsoon rains and is crucial for the cultivation of many important crops dependent on rainfall.

  • Months: June - July
  • Harvesting Time: September - October
  • Crops Grown:
    • Rice (Paddy)
    • Maize (Corn)
    • Bajra (Pearl Millet)
    • Jowar (Sorghum)
    • Soybean
    • Cotton
    • Groundnut
  • Seasonal Feature: Dependent on monsoon rainfall; vital for widespread agriculture.

Winter Season (Rabi)

Winter crops grow during cold months and are usually harvested in spring. This season is less reliant on rainfall compared to the monsoon season.

  • Months: October - November
  • Harvesting Time: March - April
  • Crops Grown:
    • Wheat
    • Barley
    • Mustard
    • Peas
    • Chana (Chickpea)
    • Oats
  • Seasonal Feature: Grows in cooler temperatures; harvested in spring.

Summer Season (Zaid)

The Summer season includes short-duration crops grown between the Rabi and Kharif seasons, mostly before the start of monsoon rains.

  • Months: March - April
  • Harvesting Time: June (before monsoon)
  • Crops Grown:
    • Watermelon
    • Muskmelon
    • Cucumber
    • Pumpkin, Bitter gourd, and other vegetables
    • Fodder Crops
  • Seasonal Feature: Short duration crops grown between monsoon and winter.

Autumn Season

The Autumn season acts as a transition phase and can involve harvesting late Kharif crops or preparing fields for winter crops.

  • Months: November - December
  • Harvesting Time: December - January
  • Crops Grown:
    • Late Kharif Rice
    • Maize
    • Vegetables suitable for cool weather
  • Seasonal Feature: Transitional phase with mild weather, important for crop rotation.
